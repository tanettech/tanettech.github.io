About

Closing the digital language divide

I'm a Cameroonian researcher associate at Carnegie Mellon University working on multilingual speech systems for languages that modern AI has largely left behind. My work spans automatic speech recognition, speech-to-speech translation, and low-resource language modeling across more than 30 African languages.

My path runs from electrical engineering to speech technology at CMU, with deployment experience across Rwanda and Kenya. I care about the full arc of the problem: curating clean speech data, adapting transformer architectures to phonetically and morphologically rich languages, and shipping models that run on the resource-constrained hardware people actually use. Working across this stack taught me that for African language technology, data availability not model design is usually the real bottleneck.

I'm persuing research programs that will advance my knowlege in : robust, data-efficient speech and language models that serve low resource and unexploited languages.

Research Interests

What I want to work on

My research sits at the intersection of speech, multilinguality, and efficiency — building recognition and translation systems that actually work for the world's underserved languages.

Speech representations

Low-resource & multilingual speech

Data-efficient training and self-supervised representations for languages with little labelled audio, where high-resource recipes don't transfer.

Efficiency

Streaming & on-device speech

Cache-based streaming, post-training quantization, and low-frame-rate coding for real-time, low-latency recognition under tight compute and bandwidth budgets.

Transfer

Cross-lingual learning

Multilingual and transfer learning so data-scarce languages benefit from related ones — especially across the Bantu group and tonal languages.

Rigor

Evaluation & responsible deployment

Benchmarks and metrics that hold up beyond high-resource settings, and speech AI deployed responsibly in real-world African contexts.

Research & Publications

Selected work

Under review · ACL ARR 2026 → EMNLP 2026 · Digital Umuganda

KinyaSpeak: cache-based streaming ASR for Kinyarwanda, English & French

First-author work — a FastConformer hybrid RNN-T/CTC streaming model (~120M params) fine-tuned on ~6,000 hours of trilingual speech, running from 0 ms real-time captioning to offline. Documents a streaming-beats-offline effect: Kinyarwanda WER of 18.1% at 1040 ms versus 20.7% offline.

🤗 Model Streaming ASR RNN-T / CTC First author
Ongoing, Under review → 2026 IEEE Spoken Language Technology Workshop· Wakanda AI · Carnegie Mellon University

Aura-ASR: multilingual speech recognition for 22 African languages

Multi-stage system (self-supervised CTC encoder → projector → Aura-1B LLM) trained on ~17,000 hours across 22 African languages, reaching state-of-the-art WER on several FLEURS, NCHLT, and BembaSpeech benchmarks and surpassing MMS-1B and OmniASR. Also built the 24-pair NLLB translation data pipeline and efficiency methods.
